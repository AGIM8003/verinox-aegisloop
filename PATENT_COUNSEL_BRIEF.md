# Patent Professional Review Brief

## Project

**VERINOX AEGISLOOP — Systems and Methods for Causally Bound Evidence-Closed Recovery of Cyber-Physical and Reconfigurable Computing Systems**

**Named author and concept originator:** Agim Haxhijaha

This brief does not request or imply a patentability conclusion. It organizes the technical disclosure for qualified professional review.

## Problem

Recovery controllers may rely on correlated evidence, act after state drift, accept digital acknowledgments as physical success, misattribute an externally caused result to their action, close before delayed instability appears, and train future behavior on an invalid recovery.

## Preferred embodiment

An eight-node reconfigurable edge fabric containing:

- a safety MCU;
- independently powered analog current or link sensing;
- authenticated digital attestation;
- a controllable power or network isolation element;
- one partially reconfigurable computing zone;
- local deterministic verifier and receipt store; and
- fault-injection equipment.

## Strongest proposed sequence

1. Build a dependency hypergraph for evidence supporting each safety claim.
2. Require a policy-defined surviving-domain cut.
3. Freeze the pre-action state and recovery candidate.
4. Generate intervention and fallback counterfactual outcome envelopes.
5. Pass proof obligations and attempted refutations.
6. Issue a one-use actuator-bound recovery bond.
7. Measure results through an independent path over immediate, stable, and delayed horizons.
8. Close only after outcome and attribution checks.
9. Reopen on later contradictory evidence.
10. Trace and contain all model or policy influence derived from revoked closure.
