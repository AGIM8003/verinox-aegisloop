## Neighboring established directions

| Direction | Established capability | AEGISLOOP differentiation sought |
|---|---|---|
| Simplex/runtime assurance | Switch to a verified or monitored safety controller | Extends through measured recovery closure, reopening, and learning influence |
| Certified control | Independently check evidence that a proposed action is safe | Adds claim-specific causal independence, dual counterfactual recovery outcomes, bonded execution, and post-action causal closure |
| Reachability and safety shields | Block or project unsafe learned actions | Recovery transaction includes physical readback, delayed closure, and revocable learning lineage |

| Remote attestation | Authenticate measured workload or device properties | Treats attestation as one evidence source with explicit dependency and freshness limits |

| Fault isolation and self-healing | Detect, isolate, reroute, restart, or reconfigure | Requires independent outcome proof and prevents false-success learning |
| Tamper-evident provenance | Preserve signed histories and dependencies | Uses superseding receipts and learning influence propagation after reopening |
| Safe or offline learning gates | Restrict unsafe policy updates | Eligibility derives from retrospective recovery closure and remains revocable |
| Machine unlearning | Remove or reduce influence of selected training data | Adds transaction lineage from physical recovery receipt to every dependent artifact |

## Research anchors

- *Certified Control* overlaps strongly with evidence assembled before action and independent checking.
- Black-Box Simplex overlaps with runtime switching and safety monitoring.
- Reachability-based safe reinforcement learning overlaps with action gating.
- Causal digital twins overlap with intervention and counterfactual reasoning.
- RATS and EAT standardize attestation roles and claims.
- SCITT and COSE receipts overlap with transparent signed evidence records.

## Candidate remaining gap

The candidate gap is the mandatory sequence combining:

1. per-claim shared-failure dependency cuts;
2. action-versus-fallback counterfactual envelopes;
3. one-use actuator-bound recovery authorization;
4. immediate, stable, and retrospective closure;
5. reopening on later contradictory evidence; and
6. containment of all downstream learning influence.

No single source found in the targeted research established that full ordered sequence. This is only an inference from the reviewed material, not a novelty finding.
